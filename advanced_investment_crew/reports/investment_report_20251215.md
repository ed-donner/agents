## 3. Portfolio Optimization  

### Modern Portfolio Theory (MPT) Overview  

MPT is an investment framework that constructs portfolios to maximize expected return for a given level of risk, or equivalently minimize risk for a given return target. It relies on mean-variance optimization using historical return estimates, volatilities, and their correlations. Key concepts include the Efficient Frontier (the set of optimal portfolios) and portfolio diversification benefits which reduce overall risk due to imperfect correlations between assets.

---

### Optimization Process  

Using asset return data from 2014–2024 with daily frequency, covariance matrices were estimated and portfolios optimized for:  
- Maximum Sharpe Ratio (reward-to-risk)  
- Minimum Volatility (lowest risk)  
Across global equity, fixed income, and alternative ETFs. Constraints included no short positions and a max 40% concentration per individual asset. Currency risk for Turkish portfolios was incorporated through USD-TRY FX volatility.

### Risk Mitigation Strategies

- **Stop-loss Orders:** For aggressively priced or volatile assets, consider 10-15% trailing stops.  
- **Liquidity Monitoring:** Avoid asset concentration in illiquid names; prioritize ETFs with high average daily volumes.

## 6. Frequently Asked Questions  

1. **How much should I invest initially?**  
   Start with an amount you are comfortable risking long-term. Diversify and build over time if uncertain, using dollar-cost averaging.  

2. **When is the best time to invest?**  
   Time in the market beats timing the market. Invest gradually if nervous about volatility.  

3. **Should I invest all at once or gradually?**  
   Gradual investing reduces timing risk, especially in volatile markets like Turkey.  

4. **How often should I check my portfolio?**  
   Quarterly reviews suffice for most investors. Monthly monitoring can help identify issues early.  

5. **What if the market crashes after I invest?**  
   Stay calm, focus on long-term goals. Diversification and hedging limit losses. Consider rebalancing to buy undervalued assets.  

6. **Can I customize the recommended allocation?**  
   Yes, tailor based on personal risk tolerance and goals, but respect diversification principles.  

7. **What about taxes?**  
   Understand local tax laws on capital gains, dividends, and interest. Use tax-advantaged accounts and tax-loss harvesting to optimize.  

8. **Should I use an advisor?**  
   Professional advice is recommended for portfolios >$500,000 or complex financial situations.  

9. **What if my risk tolerance changes?**  
   Reassess your portfolio allocation and gradually shift toward a profile matching your updated risk tolerance.  

10. **Are returns guaranteed?**  
    No investments are guaranteed. There is risk of loss including principal. Diversification and risk management reduce but do not eliminate risk.

## 7. Appendix  

### Methodology Explanation  

- **Modern Portfolio Theory (MPT):** Uses historical mean returns, variances, and covariances to construct portfolios optimizing risk-return trade-offs. Assumes return distributions approximate normality, markets are efficient, and correlations stable.  

- **Optimization Process:** Employed quadratic programming algorithms to maximize Sharpe ratio and minimize portfolio variance under no-shorting constraints. Data cleaned for outliers.  

- **Risk Metrics:** VaR and CVaR computed via historical simulation techniques over rolling windows; maximum drawdown and recovery time measured from peak-to-trough price series.

- Currency rates sourced from FXCM and ECB datasets.

### Assumptions and Limitations

- Risk estimates assume stable market regimes which may not hold in extreme conditions.  
- Currency risk particularly volatile in emerging markets like Turkey.  
- Model does not incorporate behavioral biases or changing economic policies explicitly.  
- Data limitations on emerging market ETFs and illiquid securities.

---

### Glossary  

- **VaR:** Value at Risk, maximum loss expected over a period at a given confidence level.  
- **CVaR:** Conditional VaR, expected loss conditional on losses beyond VaR threshold.  
- **Sharpe Ratio:** Risk-adjusted return measure (excess return/volatility).  
- **MPT:** Framework optimizing portfolio based on mean-variance efficiency.  
- **Beta:** Sensitivity of asset returns to market returns.  
- **Drawdown:** Peak-to-trough decline in portfolio value.
