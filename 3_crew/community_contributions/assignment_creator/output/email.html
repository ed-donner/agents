Dear Parent,

Empathy Adventure

Grade Three English Assignment

Instructions:

  1. Read each story carefully and answer the questions that follow.
  2. Choose a picture from the drawing book to illustrate your favorite story.
  3. Write a short essay (at least 50 words) on what you learned about empathy from the stories.
  4. Use vocabulary words from the box to describe your feelings and experiences.

Vocabulary Box:

  • Sympathize: to understand and share someone's feelings
  • Empathy: the ability to understand and share someone's feelings
  • Feelings: emotions or sensations in the mind
  • Friend: someone you trust and care about

Story 1:

Lily's friend, Emma, was very sad because she lost her favorite toy, Mr. Whiskers. Lily asked Emma what was wrong and Emma told her. Lily said, "I'm so sorry, Emma. That's really sad. Would you like to play with me?" Emma smiled and said, "Yes, that would make me feel better."

Question 1: What did Lily do to help Emma feel better? _______________________________

Story 2:

Max forgot to invite his friend, Olivia, to his birthday party. Olivia was very disappointed when she found out. Max felt bad and said, "Olivia, I'm really sorry. Would you like to come to my party now? I want you to be there." Olivia smiled and said, "Thank you, Max. That's very kind of you."

Question 2: What did Max do to help Olivia feel better? _______________________________

Story 3:

Sarah saw her classmate, Jack, crying during recess. Jack had fallen and hurt his knee. Sarah went to him and said, "Are you okay? Do you need some help getting up?" Jack said, "Thanks, Sarah. I'm okay now." Sarah smiled and said, "I'm glad you're okay. Do you want to be my friend?"

Question 3: What did Sarah do to help Jack feel better? _______________________________
