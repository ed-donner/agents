1. **Definition**: Gradient descent is a first-order iterative algorithm used for minimizing a differentiable multivariate function by taking repeated steps in the opposite direction of the gradient at the current point.

2. **Gradient**: The gradient of a function represents the direction of the steepest ascent. Therefore, moving in the opposite direction of the gradient leads to the steepest descent, which is the basis of the gradient descent method.

3. **Applications**: Gradient descent is particularly valuable in machine learning and artificial intelligence for minimizing cost or loss functions, which are critical for model training.

4. **Comparison with Local Search Algorithms**: Gradient descent should not be confused with local search algorithms, although both are iterative optimization methods.

5. **Historical Context**: 
   - The concept is attributed to Augustin-Louis Cauchy, who proposed it in 1847.
   - Jacques Hadamard independently proposed a similar method in 1907.
   - Haskell Curry studied the convergence properties of gradient descent for non-linear optimization problems in 1944.

6. **Variants**: A notable variant of gradient descent is stochastic gradient descent (SGD), which is widely used for training deep learning models today.
