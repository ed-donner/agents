I strongly support the motion: **there should be strict laws to regulate LLMs**.

LLMs are not ordinary software. They can persuade millions, generate falsehoods at scale, manipulate public opinion, expose private data, and amplify harm faster than any human can respond. Without strict regulation, we are essentially allowing powerful systems to operate with little accountability in education, healthcare, finance, politics, and security.

The case for strict laws is simple: **the risks are systemic, the harm is scalable, and voluntary self-regulation is not enough**. Companies have strong incentives to move fast and capture market share, even when safety is incomplete. We should not rely on goodwill when the consequences include misinformation, fraud, discrimination, deepfakes, and the erosion of trust in public institutions.

Strict laws would create basic guardrails: mandatory transparency about AI-generated content, independent safety audits, clear liability when systems cause harm, strong data privacy protections, and limits on high-risk uses. These are not barriers to innovation; they are the conditions for responsible innovation. A technology this powerful must be made safe before it is made ubiquitous.

In short, LLMs can bring enormous benefits, but only if society keeps control. **Strict laws are necessary to protect the public, preserve trust, and ensure AI serves people rather than endangering them.**
